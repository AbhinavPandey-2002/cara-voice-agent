Q: How do I register as a new patient at Greenfield Medical Centre?
A: To register visit reception in person during opening hours Monday to Friday 8am to 6:30pm. Bring photo ID such as passport or driving licence and proof of address such as a utility bill or bank statement dated within 3 months. Fill in a GMS1 registration form at reception. Registration takes up to 3 working days to process. You must live within our catchment area to register. Call reception to check if your postcode falls within our catchment area before visiting.

Q: How do I get my test results?
A: Test results are usually available within 5 to 10 working days depending on the test. Do not call before this time as results will not be available. Results can be checked via NHS app or Patient Access online. Reception can give routine results by phone during opening hours. Some results require a GP appointment to discuss. Your GP will contact you directly if results need urgent attention or further action.

Q: How do I get a sick note or fit note?
A: For sickness lasting 7 days or fewer you self certify using the SC2 self certification form available from HMRC website or your employer. A GP sick note is not required for the first 7 days. For sickness lasting more than 7 days book a GP appointment to discuss. GP will assess and issue a fit note if appropriate. Backdated sick notes are generally not issued. Sick notes cannot be issued over the phone without an appointment.

Q: How do I request a home visit?
A: Home visits are only for patients who are genuinely housebound and physically unable to attend surgery. Home visits are not available for convenience. Call reception before 10:00am if you require a home visit that day. A GP will call you back to assess whether a home visit is clinically appropriate. Home visits take place in the afternoon. Patients who can be transported to surgery by car or taxi should attend surgery as this allows better examination and treatment.

Q: How do I get a referral to a specialist?
A: Referrals to NHS specialists are made by your GP following an appointment. Book a GP appointment to discuss your condition and need for referral. GP will decide if referral is clinically appropriate. NHS referrals are made via the NHS e-referral system and you will receive a letter with details of how to book your specialist appointment. Referral waiting times vary depending on specialty and clinical priority. Private referrals can also be arranged during GP appointment if preferred.

Q: How do I make a complaint about the surgery?
A: If you have a complaint speak to the practice manager in person or write to us at the surgery address. We aim to acknowledge complaints within 3 working days and provide a full response within 20 working days. If you are not satisfied with our response you can contact NHS England or the Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman.

Q: How do I update my personal details such as address or phone number?
A: Contact reception by phone or in person to update your address, phone number, or email address. Bring proof of new address if changing address. You can also update your details on NHS app if registered for online access.

Q: Can I book an appointment for someone else?
A: You can book an appointment on behalf of a family member including children under 16. You will need to provide their name, date of birth, and reason for appointment. For patients over 16 you need their consent to book on their behalf due to patient confidentiality.

Q: How do I access my medical records?
A: You have the right to access your medical records. Submit a Subject Access Request in writing to the practice manager. We will respond within 30 days. Records are also available via NHS app for registered patients. Some records may be withheld if disclosure would cause harm.

Q: What interpreter services are available?
A: Greenfield Medical Centre can arrange professional interpreter services for patients who do not speak English. Inform reception when booking your appointment that you require an interpreter and specify the language needed. Dr Priya Patel speaks Gujarati and Hindi and can consult patients in these languages directly without an interpreter.

Q: How do I get a travel vaccination or travel health advice?
A: Book a travel health appointment with Nurse Sarah Williams at least 6 to 8 weeks before travel. Bring details of your destination, travel dates, and planned activities. Some travel vaccinations require multiple doses over several weeks. Some travel vaccinations are available free on NHS, others may need to be paid for privately. Nurse Williams will advise on all vaccinations and health precautions needed for your destination.

Q: How do I cancel or reschedule an appointment?
A: To cancel or reschedule call reception as early as possible so the slot can be offered to another patient. You can also cancel online via NHS app or Patient Access if registered. Please give at least 24 hours notice where possible. Repeated failure to attend without cancelling may result in removal from the patient list.

Q: What should I do if I need urgent advice when the surgery is closed?
A: If the surgery is closed and you need urgent medical advice call NHS 111. Available 24 hours 7 days a week. For life threatening emergencies always call 999. Do not wait until surgery opens if you are seriously unwell.

Q: Does the surgery offer telephone or video appointments?
A: Yes. Telephone and video appointments are available for conditions that do not require physical examination. When booking inform reception whether you prefer telephone, video, or in person appointment. GP will advise if in person examination is needed. Video appointments available via NHS app.
