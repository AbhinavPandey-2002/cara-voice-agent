REPEAT PRESCRIPTION REQUEST PROCESS:
Repeat prescriptions take 48 working hours to process. Do not call on the day you need medication. Submit request at least 2 working days before you run out. Request online via NHS app or Patient Access, in person at reception, or by posting the right side of your prescription slip. Prescriptions cannot be requested by telephone. Allow 48 hours before collecting from surgery or your nominated pharmacy.

REPEAT PRESCRIPTION ELIGIBILITY:
Repeat prescriptions are only available for medication already prescribed by a GP at Greenfield Medical Centre and appearing on your repeat prescription list. If medication is not on your repeat list you need a GP appointment to discuss. Check your repeat list on NHS app or Patient Access.

NEW PRESCRIPTION REQUIRES GP APPOINTMENT:
If you need medication not currently on your repeat prescription list you must book a GP appointment first. CARA cannot issue new prescriptions. A GP must assess and prescribe. Book a routine GP appointment.

CONTROLLED DRUGS PRESCRIPTION POLICY:
Controlled drugs such as morphine, codeine, diazepam, and ADHD medication cannot be requested online or by post. Patient must collect controlled drug prescriptions in person at reception with photo ID. 48 hour processing time still applies. No exceptions to this policy.

NHS PRESCRIPTION CHARGES:
Standard NHS prescription charge applies per item unless exempt. Exemptions include: under 16, aged 16 to 18 in full time education, aged 60 or over, pregnant or had baby in last 12 months, valid medical exemption certificate, valid maternity exemption certificate, receiving certain benefits. Pre-payment certificates available for patients needing multiple items per month. Ask at reception for details.

PRESCRIPTION COLLECTION:
Prescriptions collected from Greenfield Medical Centre reception during opening hours Monday to Friday 8am to 6pm and Saturday 8am to 11:30am. If you have nominated a pharmacy prescriptions are sent electronically directly. Check with pharmacy before collecting.

MEDICATION REVIEW APPOINTMENTS:
Some repeat medications require an annual medication review with GP or nurse before prescription can continue. You will be notified when review is due. Book medication review with GP or Nurse Williams depending on medication type.

EMERGENCY SUPPLY OF MEDICATION:
If you have run out of medication urgently and cannot wait 48 hours visit your local pharmacy. Pharmacists can provide emergency supply of some medications. For urgent medication needs outside surgery hours call 111 who can arrange emergency prescription if clinically necessary.

ONLINE PRESCRIPTION SERVICES:
NHS app available for repeat prescription requests. Patient Access also available. Register at reception for online access. Online requests processed same as in person. 48 hour processing time still applies.

PRESCRIPTION FOR TRAVEL ABROAD:
If travelling abroad and need additional supply of medication book GP appointment at least 4 weeks before travel. Some medications have restrictions on quantities that can be prescribed for travel. Controlled drugs require additional documentation for international travel.
